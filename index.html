About the Temple

Shri Lakshmi Ranganatha Swami Temple is a historic temple located in Mudalavitalapura, Holehonnuru hobli, Bhadravathi taluk, Shivamogga district. The temple is dedicated to Shri Lakshmi Ranganatha Swami and has been serving devotees from Mudalavitalapura and nearby villages for more than 70 years.

About

The temple is managed by a dedicated committee of nine members and comes under the Muzarai Department of the Government of Karnataka. It was originally built by our ancestors and has been maintained with devotion across generations. The temple is known for its special annual festivals, especially the grand Rathotsava and the sacred Kadekarthika celebrations, which attract devotees from Mudalavitalapura and surrounding villages.

The temple complex includes a beautiful sanctum sanctorum, a spacious hall for gatherings, and facilities for Annadana (food offerings). The serene environment and spiritual ambiance make it a perfect place for worship and meditation.

History & Legend

The temple's origins date back over 70 years, built by the forefathers of Mudalavitalapura. It has been a center of faith and devotion for the local community ever since. The temple has witnessed numerous renovations and expansions over the decades, reflecting the unwavering dedication of its devotees.

Festivals & Events

The temple celebrates many religious and cultural events throughout the year.

Brahma Rathotsava Usually held in April/March. Grand procession, cultural programs, and Annadana.
Navaratri Celebrations Nine days of special poojas, alankaras, and cultural events.
Special Poojas Sankashti, Amavasya, Ekadashi, etc..
